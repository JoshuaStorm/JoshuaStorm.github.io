Who I am

I am always curious about new opportunities to dive deeper and apply what I've learned. If you're interested in more of my formal credentials, my resume can be found here. If you think I'd be a good fit for some opportunity, please send me a quick email!

Aside from computing, I am particularly interested in music. Personally, I enjoy electronic music production, composition, and creating installations. For those interested in that side of things, see this page.

My middle name is, in fact, actually Storm. Can you blame me for parading that around? I bet if your middle name were Storm you would do the same!

What I am up to

This summer I am excited to be interning at Optiver on the Automated Trading Systems team as a software developer. If you're in Chicago and would like to chat about anything, feel free to send me an email and we can grab coffee. In my free time you will find me finishing up development on the Snyderphonics MantaMate. As well as producing new firmware for the Snyderphonics Genera, a STM37-based digital Eurorack module. And, of course, you'll find me playing with my synths and drinking coffee!

The largest non-work project I have worked on this year is Meetable: a web application for scheduling meetings easily with the help of a users Google Calendar. Last summer, I developed a subtractive synthesizer in the browser. I will be the first to say that the user-interface needs some work at the moment, but it is still a fun toy to play with.

I personally value open information and community in both programming and artistic endeavors. As such, much of my original work is licensed under GNU GPL and Creative Commons, Attribution-NonCommercial where applicable.
